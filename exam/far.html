FAR Overview & Exam Format

⏱️ Time: 4 hours

📝 Format: 50 Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) + 7 Task-Based Simulations (TBSs)

📊 Weight: MCQs 50%, TBSs 50%

🎯 Passing Score: 75

📈 Typical Pass Rate: ~45-50% (often the lowest pass rate of all sections)

⚠️ Why FAR is Hard

FAR isn't conceptually the hardest — it's the broadest. It covers more topics than any other section. Many candidates underestimate how much material they need to know and run out of study time.

Key Topics & Content Areas

The AICPA breaks FAR into 4 content areas. Here's what you need to know for each:

Area I: Financial Reporting (30-35%)

  • Conceptual Framework & Standard-Setting — FASB ASC structure, qualitative characteristics, elements of financial statements
  • Financial Statement Accounts — Cash, receivables, inventory (FIFO/LIFO/Weighted Avg), PP&E, intangibles, investments
  • Revenue Recognition (ASC 606) — 5-step model: identify contract → performance obligations → transaction price → allocation → recognition
  • Income Statement & Comprehensive Income — Single-step vs multi-step, discontinued operations, OCI components

Area II: Select Balance Sheet Accounts (30-35%)

  • Leases (ASC 842) — Operating vs finance leases, lessee & lessor accounting, right-of-use assets
  • Bonds & Long-Term Debt — Bond pricing, amortization (effective interest method), extinguishment
  • Equity — Common/preferred stock, treasury stock, dividends, stock splits
  • Pensions & Post-Retirement Benefits — Defined benefit vs defined contribution, PBO, plan assets

Area III: Select Transactions (20-25%)

  • Business Combinations — Acquisition method, goodwill calculation, non-controlling interest
  • Consolidations — Intercompany eliminations, consolidated financial statements
  • Equity Method Investments — When to use, how to calculate
  • Derivatives & Hedging — Fair value hedges, cash flow hedges
  • Foreign Currency — Translation vs remeasurement, functional currency determination

Area IV: State & Local Governments (10-15%)

  • Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) — How it differs from FASB
  • Fund Accounting — Governmental (GRaSPP), Proprietary (SE), Fiduciary (PAPI) funds
  • Modified Accrual vs Full Accrual — When each applies
  • Government-Wide Financial Statements — Statement of Net Position, Statement of Activities
  • Non-Profit Accounting — Net asset classifications, contributions, donor restrictions

🎯 High-Yield Tip

Governmental & NFP accounting is only 10-15% of the exam, but many candidates skip it because it feels "different." DON'T. These questions are often more straightforward than ASC 842 lease calculations — they're easy points if you study them.

Hardest FAR Topics (and How to Tackle Them)

  1. Leases (ASC 842) — Practice both lessee AND lessor perspectives. Draw the timeline. Understand the difference between operating and finance classification criteria.
  2. Bonds & Effective Interest Method — Master the amortization table. If you can do bond problems in your sleep, you'll pick up easy points.
  3. Consolidations — Focus on the CAR IN BIG mnemonic for acquisition method. Practice eliminations until they feel mechanical.
  4. Pensions — Understand the 5 components of pension expense: SIR AGE (Service cost, Interest cost, Return on plan assets, Amortization of prior service, Gains/losses, Existing amortization of transition)
  5. Governmental Fund Types — GRaSPP vs SE-PAPI. Know which uses modified accrual vs full accrual. This ALWAYS shows up.

Pro Tips for Passing FAR

  1. Start with FAR first. It's the hardest and most time-consuming. If you pass FAR, the psychological boost is huge. Plus, FAR concepts carry over to AUD and BAR.
  2. MCQ volume matters. Aim for 2,000+ practice MCQs before exam day. This isn't about memorization — it's about pattern recognition.
  3. Don't get stuck on one topic. If leases aren't clicking after 2 days, move on and come back. FAR is broad — you can't afford to obsess over one area.
  4. Use the authoritative literature during TBSs. The FASB codification is available during simulations. Practice looking up answers in it — it's a skill.
  5. Time management is critical. Budget ~1.5 min per MCQ, ~20 min per TBS. Watch the clock.
  6. JEs everywhere. If you can write the journal entry, you understand the transaction. If you can't — go back and study the JE.

Common FAR Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake #1: Memorizing Instead of Understanding

You can't memorize your way through FAR — there's too much. Focus on WHY the accounting treatment exists, not just WHAT it is.

❌ Mistake #2: Skipping Governmental/NFP

"It's only 10-15%" — and if you skip it, those lost points could be the difference between a 74 and a 75. Study it. It's not that hard.

❌ Mistake #3: Neglecting TBS Practice

TBSs are 50% of your score. Don't spend all your time on MCQs. Practice simulations — especially the ones with multiple exhibits.

❌ Mistake #4: No Mock Exams

You need at least 2 full-length mock exams to build stamina. 4 hours is a marathon — train for it.
