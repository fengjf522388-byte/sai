You must choose one Discipline section. Here's how to make the right decision for your career.

The Discipline Model Explained

Under the CPA Evolution model (effective 2024+), every candidate must pass:

  • 3 Core sections (required): FAR, AUD, REG
  • 1 Discipline section (your choice): BAR, ISC, or TCP

The Discipline you choose demonstrates a deeper competency in a specific area. Combined with your Core knowledge, it signals expertise to employers.

💡 Strategic Insight

Your Discipline choice doesn't lock you into a career path. Many Big 4 firms encourage candidates to choose the Discipline that aligns with their strongest Core section. Having a CPA license matters far more than which Discipline you picked.

📊 BAR — Business Analysis & Reporting

Best for: Future CFOs, financial analysts, corporate accountants

Natural follow-up to: FAR (heavy overlap)

Content focus: Advanced financial analysis, risk management, capital structure, data analytics, financial planning

Key Topics

  • Financial Statement Analysis — Advanced ratio analysis, trend analysis, forecasting
  • Business Valuation — DCF, market multiples, asset-based approaches
  • Risk Management — Enterprise risk management (COSO ERM), financial risk, operational risk
  • Capital Structure & Financing — WACC, optimal capital structure, debt vs equity
  • Data Analytics & Visualization — Data extraction, transformation, analysis techniques
  • Performance Measurement — Balanced scorecard, KPIs, variance analysis

⚠️ BAR Difficulty

BAR is considered the most challenging Discipline because it builds directly on FAR concepts and adds advanced financial analysis. If FAR was a struggle, BAR will be harder.

💻 ISC — Information Systems & Controls

Best for: IT auditors, tech-savvy accountants, future CTOs/CIOs

Natural follow-up to: AUD (controls and processes overlap)

Content focus: IT governance, cybersecurity, data management, SOC engagements, system controls

Key Topics

  • IT Governance — COBIT framework, IT policies, IT strategic planning
  • Cybersecurity & Information Security — CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), security controls, incident response
  • System Development & Implementation — SDLC, change management, system testing
  • SOC Engagements — SOC 1 vs SOC 2 vs SOC 3, Type I vs Type II reports
  • Data Management — Database concepts, data governance, data quality
  • IT Controls — General controls vs application controls, access controls, segregation of duties

🎯 ISC: Often the "Easiest" Discipline

Many candidates report ISC as the most manageable Discipline, especially if they have any technology background or found AUD intuitive. The content is more conceptual and less calculation-intensive than BAR.

🧾 TCP — Tax Compliance & Planning

Best for: Tax professionals, estate planners, future tax partners

Natural follow-up to: REG (heavy overlap with entity taxation)

Content focus: Advanced individual tax planning, entity tax planning, estate & gift taxation, international tax

Key Topics

  • Advanced Individual Tax Planning — Timing strategies, income shifting, AMT planning
  • Entity Tax Planning — Choice of entity analysis, compensation planning, retirement planning
  • Estate & Gift Taxation — Advanced transfer tax planning, trusts, GRATs, family limited partnerships
  • International Taxation — Foreign tax credits, transfer pricing basics, PFICs, FBAR/FATCA
  • State & Local Tax (SALT) — Nexus, apportionment, multi-state taxation
  • IRS Practice & Procedures — Advanced topics in tax controversy, penalty abatement

🎯 TCP: Best if You Love Tax

If REG was your favorite Core section and you're pursuing a tax career, TCP is the natural choice. The content is a direct extension of REG's entity taxation topics.

How to Choose: Decision Framework

Ask yourself these 4 questions:

  1. Which Core section did you feel strongest in? Pick the Discipline that matches.
  2. What's your career direction? Corporate finance → BAR. IT audit/tech → ISC. Tax → TCP. Undecided? ISC is the safest generalist choice.
  3. How much time do you have? ISC generally requires the least study time. BAR requires the most.
  4. What content style suits you? Calculations and analysis → BAR. Concepts and frameworks → ISC. Rules and thresholds → TCP.

🏆 If You're Unsure: Pick ISC

ISC is the most accessible Discipline with the widest career applicability. IT controls and data management knowledge is valuable in every accounting role. And if you change your mind later — no one asks which Discipline you took, only that you have your CPA.
