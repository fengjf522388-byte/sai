REG Overview & Exam Format

⏱️ Time: 4 hours

📝 Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

📊 Weight: MCQs 50%, TBSs 50%

📈 Typical Pass Rate: ~55-62% (generally the highest pass rate)

🧠 What Makes REG Different

REG is rules and phase-outs heavy. You need to know specific dollar amounts, percentages, and eligibility thresholds. Unlike AUD's judgment calls, REG has clear right and wrong answers — which many candidates find easier.

Key Topics & Content Areas

Area I: Ethics, Professional Responsibilities & Federal Tax Procedures (10-20%)

  • Circular 230 — Rules governing practice before the IRS, due diligence, contingent fees
  • Treasury Department Rules — AICPA Statements on Standards for Tax Services (SSTS)
  • IRS Procedures — Statute of limitations (3 years / 6 years / no limit for fraud), audit process
  • Penalties — Accuracy-related, fraud, failure-to-file, failure-to-pay

Area II: Business Law (15-25%)

  • Contracts — Formation (COAL), the UCC vs common law, breach, remedies, Statute of Frauds
  • Agency — Actual vs apparent authority, fiduciary duties, respondeat superior
  • Business Structures — Sole proprietorship, general/limited partnership, LLC, corporations
  • Bankruptcy — Chapter 7 (liquidation) vs 11 (reorganization) vs 13 (wage earner plan)
  • Secured Transactions — Article 9, attachment, perfection, priority

Area III: Federal Taxation of Property Transactions (5-15%)

  • Basis Calculation — Cost basis, adjusted basis, gift basis, inherited basis (stepped up to FMV)
  • Capital Gains & Losses — Short-term vs long-term, netting rules, $3,000 deduction limit
  • Like-Kind Exchanges (§1031) — Real property only (post-TCJA), boot, deferred gain
  • Depreciation — MACRS, §179 expensing, bonus depreciation

Area IV: Federal Taxation of Individuals (20-30%)

  • Filing Status — Single, MFJ, MFS, HOH, Qualifying Widow(er)
  • Income Inclusions & Exclusions — Wages, investments, gifts, inheritances, life insurance, muni bond interest
  • Adjustments (Above-the-Line) — IRA contributions, HSA, student loan interest, self-employment tax deduction
  • Itemized Deductions (Below-the-Line) — Medical, SALT ($10K cap), mortgage interest, charitable contributions
  • Credits vs Deductions — Credits reduce tax dollar-for-dollar; deductions reduce taxable income
  • AMT — Alternative Minimum Tax basics

Area V: Federal Taxation of Entities (20-30%)

  • C Corporations — Double taxation, dividends received deduction (DRD), charitable contribution limits
  • S Corporations — Pass-through, eligibility requirements (100 shareholders max, one class of stock), basis adjustments
  • Partnerships — Formation (§721), inside vs outside basis, special allocations, guaranteed payments
  • LLCs — Default classification (disregarded for single-member, partnership for multi-member), check-the-box
  • Estate & Gift Tax — Annual exclusion ($18,000 for 2024), lifetime exemption (~$13M+), unlimited marital deduction

Tax Basis Rules — Quick Reference

ScenarioBasis
PurchaseCost (purchase price + expenses)
Gift (gain calculation)Donor's adjusted basis (carryover)
Gift (loss calculation)Lower of: Donor's basis OR FMV at date of gift
InheritanceFMV at date of death (or alternate valuation date)
Like-Kind ExchangeFMV of property received − deferred gain + deferred loss
Partnership InterestCash contributed + adjusted basis of property + share of liabilities
S Corporation StockInitial basis + income items − distributions − losses

🎯 High-Yield: Basis is Everything

An enormous proportion of REG questions ultimately come down to correctly calculating basis. If you can confidently calculate basis for individuals, partnerships, S-corps, and C-corps in every scenario — you're halfway to passing.

Most candidates need 80-120 hours. 8-week plan:

WeekFocus
1-2Individual Taxation: income, adjustments, deductions, credits, filing status
3-4Property Transactions: basis, gains/losses, like-kind exchanges, depreciation
5Entity Taxation: C-corp, S-corp, partnerships, LLCs
6Business Law: contracts, agency, bankruptcy, secured transactions
7Ethics, Circular 230, Procedures, Comprehensive Review
8Mock Exams, Weak Areas, Basis Drill Blitz

Pro Tips for Passing REG

  1. Basis, basis, basis. If you can calculate basis for any entity type in any scenario, you're golden.
  2. Know the phase-out thresholds. The exam expects you to know common phase-outs. Focus on: passive activity loss limits, IRA deduction phase-outs, child tax credit thresholds.
  3. Credits > Deductions. A $1 credit reduces tax by $1. A $1 deduction reduces tax by your marginal rate × $1. Know which is which.
  4. Business law is free points. It's only 15-25% but the questions are more straightforward than tax. Don't skip it.
  5. Practice TBSs with tax forms. REG simulations often involve filling out tax forms or schedules. Get comfortable with the format.
  6. Memorize Circular 230 basics. The ethics portion is limited scope but always tested. Due diligence, conflict of interest, contingent fees — know the rules.

❌ Common REG Mistake: Confusing Entity Taxation

C-corps pay tax at the entity level (double taxation). S-corps and partnerships are pass-through. The tax implications of distributions, liquidations, and contributions are completely different. Drill these scenarios until you can't get them wrong.
