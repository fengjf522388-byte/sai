🧠 Memory Aids & Mnemonics

The CPA exam rewards recall speed. These mnemonics help you remember key frameworks under pressure:

AUD: Audit Report Components — "TID CAR GOP"

Title, Introductory paragraph, Date → Critical audit matters, Auditor's responsibility, Responsibility of management → Going concern (if applicable), Opinion, PCAOB/Signature

FAR: Governmental Funds — "GRaSPP"

General, Special Revenue, Debt Service, Capital Projects, Permanent funds. These use Modified Accrual accounting.

FAR: Proprietary & Fiduciary Funds — "SE-PAPI"

Internal Service, Enterprise (Proprietary) → Pension trust, Agency, Private-purpose trust, Investment trust (Fiduciary). These use Full Accrual.

REG: Contract Formation — "COAL"

Consideration, Offer, Acceptance, Legal purpose — the four elements of a valid contract.

COSO Internal Control Framework — "CRIME"

Control environment, Risk assessment, Information & communication, Monitoring, Existing control activities. (Note: CRIME is an older framework; newer one uses different organization, but the mnemonic still helps)

🎯 Study Strategies & Methodology

01

Active Recall Over Passive Reading

Don't just read. Test yourself continuously. Every MCQ you answer is learning.

02

Spaced Repetition

Review topics on days 1, 3, 7, and 21. The science of long-term retention.

03

Understand Why, Not Just What

For every wrong answer, read the explanation for ALL answer choices — right and wrong.

04

Simulate Exam Conditions

Take full-length mock exams under timed, Prometric-like conditions at least twice per section.
