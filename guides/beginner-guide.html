🎓 Complete Beginner's Guide to Honor of Kings (2025)

New to Honor of Kings? This guide covers everything you need to start winning — from the basics of the map to climbing your first ranked tiers.

🗺️ The Map & Lanes

Honor of Kings is a 5v5 MOBA on a three-lane map. Each player takes one of five roles:

  • Clash Lane (Top): Solo lane for fighters/tanks. Focus on trading and split pushing.
  • Mid Lane: Mages control the center of the map. Rotate to help side lanes.
  • Farm Lane (Bottom): Marksmen farm gold here to become late-game carries.
  • Jungle: Clear jungle camps, gank lanes, secure objectives (Tyrant/Tower Lord).
  • Roamer (Support): Protect the team, provide vision, and roam between lanes.

🎯 Objectives: What Actually Wins Games

Kills are nice — objectives win games. Priority order:

  • 1. Towers: Push them. This is how you win. Nothing else matters if towers stand.
  • 2. Tyrant (early) / Tower Lord (late): Global buffs that snowball your entire team.
  • 3. Jungle Buffs: Red (damage + slow) and Blue (mana regen + CDR). Control them.
  • 4. Kills: Only useful if they lead to objectives. Don't chase kills blindly.
💡 Golden Rule: After winning a teamfight, ALWAYS push a tower or take an objective. Don't just recall and shop.

🏆 Rank System

Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond → Master → Grandmaster → Mythic

Each tier has sub-divisions (I, II, III). Win to gain stars, lose to drop them. You need 3-5 stars per sub-division. Reaching Diamond puts you in the top ~30% of players.

🚀 Quick Climbing Tips

  • Learn 2-3 heroes per role before touching ranked
  • Mute toxic teammates. Mental game matters more than mechanics
  • Watch the minimap every 3 seconds. Awareness > mechanics
  • Don't play ranked when tilted. Two losses in a row = take a break
  • Learn from every death. Ask: "Why did I die?" and don't repeat it
