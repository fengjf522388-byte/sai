How to Build a CPA Study Schedule That Actually Works

A realistic plan for candidates working full-time — with built-in flexibility.

Study Planning · 4 min read

🎯 TL;DR

Aim for 15-20 hours per week for 8-10 weeks per section. Split into 2 hours on weekdays + 5-6 hours on weekends. Include buffer weeks. Don't study 7 days a week — you'll burn out.

The 80/20 of CPA Study Scheduling

Most failed CPA attempts aren't because candidates aren't smart enough. They fail because they run out of time or burn out. A good schedule prevents both.

Here are the 3 fatal scheduling mistakes:

  1. Unrealistic daily targets — "I'll study 4 hours every day after work" (you won't)
  2. No buffer weeks — Life happens. If your schedule has zero slack, one bad week derails everything.
  3. Studying 7 days a week — Your brain needs breaks to consolidate learning. No rest = diminished returns.

The Ideal Weekly Template

For a candidate working full-time (9-5):

DayStudy TimeFocus
Monday2 hours (7-9 PM)Watch lectures, new material
Tuesday2 hours (7-9 PM)MCQ practice on Monday's material
Wednesday2 hours (7-9 PM)New material + MCQs
Thursday2 hours (7-9 PM)MCQ review, re-do wrong answers
FridayOFFRest. Seriously. Rest.
Saturday5-6 hours (8 AM-2 PM)Heavy session: new material, TBS practice, review
Sunday2-3 hours (morning)Light review, plan next week, weak area focus

Total: 15-17 hours/week

8-Week Study Plan Per Section

PhaseWeeksWhat to Do
Learn1-5Watch lectures, read textbooks, do MCQs after each module. Target: 70%+ on first-pass MCQs.
Review6-7Comprehensive review, re-do ALL flagged/wrong MCQs, take 2 full mock exams.
BufferBuilt into planOne "catch-up week" if you fall behind. If not needed — extra review!
FinalWeek 8Final review: weak area MCQ blitz, 1 more mock exam, rest day before exam.

Anti-Burnout Rules

  1. One rest day per week, minimum. No exceptions.
  2. After every 50 minutes of study, take a 10-minute break. Pomodoro method works.
  3. If you hit your weekly hour target by Friday, take Saturday off too. Reward yourself.
  4. Don't study the night before the exam. Light review only. Sleep is more valuable.
  5. If you're consistently < 60% on MCQs, slow down. You're not retaining. Go back.

💡 The 2-Day Rule

Never go more than 2 consecutive days without studying. Momentum is everything. If you miss Monday, study Tuesday. Miss Tuesday? You MUST study Wednesday. After 3+ days off, motivation plummets and restarting becomes exponentially harder.
