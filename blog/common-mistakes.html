10 Common CPA Exam Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

Learn from others' failures so you don't repeat them.

Exam Tips · 5 min read

The CPA exam has a pass rate around 45-55%. That means roughly half of all candidates fail each section. Most failures aren't due to lack of intelligence — they're due to avoidable mistakes in how people study and take the exam.

Here are the 10 most common ones, and exactly how to avoid them.

❌ #1: Not Having a Study Plan

Winging it doesn't work. You need a structured plan with weekly goals, checkpoint assessments, and a clear timeline. Fix: Build an 8-10 week plan per section with specific modules assigned to specific weeks. Track your progress.

❌ #2: Memorizing Instead of Understanding

You can memorize all the journal entries for leases — but if you don't understand WHY a lease is classified as operating vs finance, you'll freeze when the question is slightly different. Fix: For every topic, ask "Why does this accounting treatment exist?" before memorizing the mechanics.

❌ #3: Skipping TBS Practice

Task-Based Simulations are 50% of your score. Many candidates spend 90% of their time on MCQs and bomb the TBSs. Fix: Practice at least 2-3 TBSs per study module. Take full mock exams that include timed TBS sections.

❌ #4: Not Using the Authoritative Literature

During the exam, you have access to the FASB codification, auditing standards, and tax code. Many TBS answers can be found directly in the literature if you know how to search. Fix: Practice using the authoritative literature during study — it's a skill that can save you 5-10 points.

❌ #5: Poor Time Management on Exam Day

Spending 2 minutes per MCQ is fine. Spending 4 minutes per MCQ means you'll have 30 minutes for all 7 TBSs — certain failure. Fix: Budget 1.5 hours for MCQs, 2 hours for TBSs, 30 minutes buffer. If you're behind at the 2-hour mark, start guessing on remaining MCQs and move to TBSs.

❌ #6: Not Taking Mock Exams

CPA exam sections are 4 hours long. That's a marathon. If you've never sat for 4 hours of continuous testing, exam day will be a shock. Fix: Take at least 2 full-length timed mock exams per section. Simulate Prometric conditions: no phone, no snacks, one 15-minute break.

❌ #7: Ignoring Weak Areas

It's human nature: we study what we're good at because it feels rewarding. But your score comes from improving weak areas, not polishing strengths. Fix: After each practice set, identify your worst 3 topics and spend the next session drilling them exclusively.

❌ #8: Studying Passively

Watching lecture videos without doing MCQs = passive learning. Reading the textbook without testing yourself = passive learning. Your brain retains almost nothing from passive exposure. Fix: After every lecture or chapter, immediately do 20-30 MCQs. Active recall is the only way learning sticks.

❌ #9: Not Reading Wrong Answer Explanations

Getting a question right and moving on teaches you nothing about WHY the other answers were wrong. Those "why it's wrong" explanations contain massive learning value. Fix: For every question you answer — right OR wrong — read the full explanation for all answer choices.

❌ #10: Burning Out Before Exam Day

Studying 30+ hours the week before your exam depletes your mental reserves exactly when you need them most. You walk into Prometric exhausted. Fix: Taper your study: the final week should be lighter (2-3 hours/day of review), with the day before exam entirely off or 1 hour max of light review.

🏆 The #1 Meta-Mistake

Candidates underestimate how much the CPA exam tests stamina, not just knowledge. 4 hours of sustained concentration is the real challenge. Train for it like you'd train for a marathon — build endurance over weeks, not days.
