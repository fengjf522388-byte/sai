Best CPA Exam Order for 2026

Which section should you take first? The data-backed answer — and why it matters.

Updated: 2026 · Study Strategy · 5 min read

🎯 TL;DR

FAR first is the best strategy for most candidates. It's the hardest and most time-consuming. If you pass FAR, you've proven you can pass the rest. And FAR concepts carry over to AUD and BAR — making those sections easier.

Why Exam Order Matters

The CPA exam has an 18-month rolling window: once you pass your first section, the clock starts. You must pass the remaining 3 sections within 18 months, or your first section expires.

Your exam order affects:

  • Psychology — Pass the hard one first = confidence boost. Fail the hard one first = demoralizing.
  • Knowledge Transfer — Some sections naturally build on others.
  • Time Efficiency — Studying overlapping content saves total study hours.
  • 18-Month Window Strategy — Don't let your hardest section expire.

Strategy #1: FAR First 🥇 (Recommended)

Order: FAR → AUD → REG → Discipline | Total Time: ~450 hours across 4 sections

Why This Works

  • FAR is the most time-consuming (100-150 hours). Get it done while motivation is highest.
  • FAR concepts carry over: AUD tests FAR topics from an audit perspective. BAR (if you choose it) builds directly on FAR.
  • Passing FAR gives you a massive psychological win early.
  • If your 18-month window starts expiring, it's better to retake AUD or REG — not FAR.

⚠️ Risk

If you struggle deeply with FAR and fail multiple times, it could delay your entire timeline and eat into the 18-month window before you've passed a single section.

Strategy #2: REG First 🥈 (Best for Tax Professionals)

Order: REG → TCP → FAR → AUD | Best for: Tax accountants who live and breathe REG topics daily

Why This Works

  • If you work in tax, REG leverages your existing knowledge — making it the "easiest" first section for you.
  • TCP (Tax Compliance & Planning discipline) flows naturally after REG.
  • Builds confidence before tackling the monster (FAR).

Strategy #3: AUD First 🥉 (Risky but Works for Some)

Order: AUD → FAR → REG → Discipline | Best for: Auditors who think in "audit terms" naturally

⚠️ Warning

AUD tests concepts that draw from FAR (like revenue recognition and lease accounting). Without FAR knowledge, some AUD TBSs will be harder. Only try this if you're an experienced auditor.

Which Discipline to Pair With Your Core

Core Taken FirstBest Discipline PairingWhy
FARBARBAR is essentially FAR Part 2 — heavy overlap in financial analysis
AUDISCBoth focus on controls, processes, and frameworks

The Verdict

For 95% of candidates, the optimal order is:

  1. FAR (100-150 hours, hardest, build momentum)
  2. AUD (80-120 hours, overlaps with FAR, second hardest conceptually)
  3. REG (80-120 hours, rules-based, highest pass rate)
  4. Discipline (60-90 hours, your choice — furthest from expiration)

The logic is simple: put the hardest sections earliest (when your 18-month clock has maximum runway), and save the shortest/easiest Discipline for last.
