CPA Exam Structure: Core + Discipline Model

The CPA Exam uses a Core + Discipline model. Every candidate must pass 4 sections total:

  • 3 Core Sections (required for everyone): FAR, AUD, REG
  • 1 Discipline Section (choose one): BAR, ISC, or TCP

You must pass all 4 sections within an 18-month rolling window from the date you pass your first section.

🧠 Quick Memory Aid

Think of it as: F.A.R.T. or F.A.R. + 1
FAR + AUD + REG = your 3 Core. Then pick your Discipline: BAR, ISC, or TCP.

All 6 Exam Sections Detailed

💰 FAR — Financial Accounting & Reporting (Core)

Time: 4 hours | Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

Content: Financial statement accounts, select transactions, state & local governments. This is typically considered the hardest section due to its breadth.

Key Topics: GAAP, IFRS differences, consolidations, leases, bonds, pensions, NFP & governmental accounting, revenue recognition, equity method, business combinations.

🔍 AUD — Auditing & Attestation (Core)

Time: 4 hours | Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

Content: Audit planning, internal controls, evidence gathering, reporting. Heavy on professional judgment and skepticism.

Key Topics: Audit risk, materiality, internal control frameworks (COSO), audit reports (unmodified/qualified/adverse/disclaimer), SSAE/SSARS engagements, professional ethics.

📖 Full AUD Study Guide →

⚖️ REG — Regulation (Core)

Time: 4 hours | Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

Content: Federal taxation, business law, ethics, and professional responsibilities. Heavy on rules and thresholds.

Key Topics: Individual taxation, corporate taxation, partnership taxation, estate & gift tax, business law (contracts, UCC), CPA professional responsibilities, IRS procedures.

📖 Full REG Study Guide →

📊 BAR — Business Analysis & Reporting (Discipline)

Time: 4 hours | Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

Content: Advanced financial analysis, risk management, capital structure, and data analytics. Best for: candidates interested in corporate finance/FP&A.

Time: 4 hours | Format: 50 MCQs + 7 TBSs

Content: IT governance, cybersecurity, SOC engagements, data management. Best for: tech-savvy candidates or those in IT audit.

Exam Format & Question Types

Each section contains two types of questions:

  • Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) — 50 per section, divided into two testlets of 25
  • Task-Based Simulations (TBSs) — 7 per section, practical case-study scenarios requiring analysis, journal entries, research, or calculations

The exam uses multi-stage adaptive testing: performance on the first MCQ testlet determines the difficulty of the second testlet.

⚠️ Important: Time Management

Many candidates spend too long on MCQs and run out of time for TBSs. TBSs are worth 50% of your score in Core sections and 50% in Disciplines. Budget your time carefully.

How the CPA Exam is Scored

  • Scoring Scale: 0-99, with 75 required to pass
  • MCQ Weight: 50% of total score
  • TBS Weight: 50% of total score
  • No penalty for wrong answers — always guess if unsure
  • Results are typically released on a rolling score release schedule

💡 Pro Tip

The CPA Exam is not curved. A 75 means you demonstrated minimum competency — not that you beat 75% of other test-takers. Focus on mastering the material, not on the competition.

Testing Windows & Timeline

As of 2025-2026:

  • CPA Exam sections are available for continuous testing throughout the year
  • You can retake a failed section after receiving your score
  • You have 18 months to pass all 4 sections after passing your first one
  • Most candidates take 6-18 months to pass all sections

What to Expect on Test Day

  1. Arrive early — at least 30 minutes before your appointment
  2. Bring two forms of ID — one must be government-issued with photo and signature
  3. No personal items allowed — lockers provided at Prometric centers
  4. You get a 15-minute break (optional) between testlets
  5. Scratch paper and calculator provided — no personal calculators allowed
  6. Total time in testing center: ~5 hours including check-in and breaks
