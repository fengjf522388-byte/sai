The 3 E's: Education, Exam, Experience

To become a licensed CPA in any U.S. state, you must satisfy the "Three E's":

  1. Education — Typically 150 semester credit hours with specific accounting coursework
  2. Exam — Pass all 4 sections of the Uniform CPA Examination
  3. Experience — 1-2 years of accounting work verified by a licensed CPA

💡 Key Insight

Each state board sets its own requirements. You don't have to get licensed in the state where you took the exam — many candidates sit for the exam through a state with flexible requirements, then transfer their license later.

Education Requirements: 150 Credit Hours

Most states require 150 semester credit hours of college education — that's typically 30 hours beyond a standard bachelor's degree (120 hours). This usually means completing a master's degree or additional coursework.

Typical Coursework Requirements:

  • Accounting Courses: 24-33 semester hours in accounting subjects (Financial, Managerial, Auditing, Tax, etc.)
  • Business Courses: 24-39 semester hours in general business subjects (Finance, Marketing, Management, Economics, etc.)
  • Ethics Coursework: Some states require specific ethics/professional responsibility courses

⚠️ Common Pitfall

Not all accounting courses count! States are specific about what qualifies. For example, "QuickBooks" courses or introductory bookkeeping may not count toward your accounting credit requirements. Check with your state board before enrolling.

CPA Exam Eligibility

To sit for the CPA exam (not necessarily get licensed), most states require:

  • 120-150 credit hours (some states allow you to test at 120)
  • Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution
  • Specific accounting and business coursework
  • Application through NASBA's CPA Examination Services (CPAES)

After applying, you'll receive a Notice to Schedule (NTS) valid for 6 months. You must schedule and take your exam sections within this window.

Work Experience Requirements

After passing the exam, you need verified work experience to get your license:

  • Duration: Typically 1 year (2,000 hours), some states require 2 years
  • Type: Must involve accounting, attest, auditing, tax, or consulting skills
  • Supervision: Generally must be under a licensed CPA who verifies your experience
  • Timing: Can be completed before, during, or after the exam (varies by state)
  • Non-Public Accounting: Many states accept industry, government, or academic accounting experience

Ethics Exam Requirements

Many states require passing an ethics exam as part of licensing:

  • AICPA Professional Ethics Exam — self-study course + open-book exam (required by ~35 states)
  • State-specific ethics exams — some states have their own ethics requirements beyond the AICPA exam
  • The ethics exam is separate from the CPA exam and is taken after passing all 4 sections

International Candidates

If you earned your education outside the U.S., you'll need:

  • Credential evaluation from a NASBA-approved evaluation service (NIES, NASBA International Evaluation Services, or approved alternatives)
  • Equivalent education to the 150-credit-hour requirement
  • English proficiency may be required by some states
  • Some jurisdictions participate in international testing (select Prometric centers in Asia, Middle East, Europe, and Latin America)
